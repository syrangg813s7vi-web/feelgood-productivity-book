So, one simple way to stay more consistent with your goals in 2026 is by building systems around the goal that you want to achieve. [music] And I think of systems in four ways. Firstly, I want a reminder system. I need a way for me to remind myself about what my goal actually is. So, for example, I have my vision board as like my desktop wallpaper and I have a daily journaling thing that asks me to like literally write down what are my goals and that reminds me what I actually wanted to do. Secondly, there is accountability. It's super super helpful when you're trying to achieve any goal to have some kind of accountability system baked [music] into it, especially if you know you're going to struggle. So this is like a WhatsApp group with friends or some kind of coach or mentor that you hold yourself accountable to. Thirdly, you want some kind of tracking system behind the goal. When students in our lifestyle business academy, for example, you know, these guys are all working towards building businesses that give them financial freedom. And so we give them a Google sheet where every single week they have to track how many hours they worked in their business, how many posts they made on social media, how many like sales conversations they had, how many sales they got. and they have to submit that every week as part of a tracking system. And that is amazingly useful because by just giving yourself visibility about the numbers without like lying about it, you're massively more likely to achieve the goal. And finally, some kind of system for time blocking because if you don't like create the containers of time for you to actually do the work it takes to achieve the goal, you're probably not going to achieve the goal. And so at the very least, you should at least have a few calendar events. These are appointments with yourself where you know you're going to be working on that specific goal.
