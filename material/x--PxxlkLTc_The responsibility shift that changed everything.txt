The realization for me was was weirdly on weekend shifts I felt weirdly more energized afterwards which is kind of weird because weekend shifts as a doctor are like more busy because there's fewer there's fewer staff there's more patients more emergencies is going on but I felt weirdly energized and I realized it's because on weekend shifts I was taking responsibility and I was taking more ownership of the things that I was doing. The way I was approaching my patients was thinking the buck kind of ends with me because I don't want to ring up the consultant. I'm going to chase up these blood results. I'm going to call radiology. I'm going to do the things. And so I was working harder. I felt like I owned the process of what I was doing. Suddenly, I had more energy and work became more fun and the shifts absolutely flew by to the point that I was like swapping with colleagues to be like, "Hey, can I work a weekend?" Cuz I was like, "It's so much more fun working on a weekend.
