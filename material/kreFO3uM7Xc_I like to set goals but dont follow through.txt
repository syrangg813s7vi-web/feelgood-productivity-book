We have a question here. I am great at vision and planning, but I struggle to take action. How can I grow that skill? So, basically, I have a method for all sorts of goal achievement. It's called the GPS method. Goal, plan, and system. So, it sounds like this person is good at the goal part, i.e. they're good at knowing what they want, which is fantastic. And it also sounds like they're good at the planning part. What is the plan that's actually going to get you to the goal? But it sounds like they're not very good at the system part. The system part is what do I actually need to do every day or every week to help me stick to the plan? Now, generally, when it comes to the system component, there are three things I like to think about. The first one is tracking. Generally, being able to track your progress towards a goal and looking at that number every single day drastically increases your chances of actually achieving that goal. So, I would ask our friend who asked the question, what way are you tracking your progress towards those goals that you claim to have? The second is accountability. So, in general, we are very bad at motivating ourselves or even staying disciplined enough to take consistent action for long enough to see results. And so, what I ask our friend is, can we build any accountability mechanisms or systems into your life? An accountability buddy or a group of people that are doing a similar thing. Could you join some kind of online community or mentorship program where you feel like you're being held accountable? And the final thing is reminders. It's very easy to set goals and to make plans for them, but honestly, life gets busy. Sometimes we can just forget that we actually wanted to do the thing. Do you have time blocks on your calendar to work on the thing that you said you were going to do? Generally, if we can incorporate tracking, accountability, and reminders into our life, it drastically increases our chances of actually taking action on the goals that we set our minds
