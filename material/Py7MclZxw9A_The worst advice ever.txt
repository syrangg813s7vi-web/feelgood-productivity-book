The worst piece of advice that I see given online a lot is the idea that you should just be yourself. I think this is a total freaking myth. Like, what does being yourself even mean? The person that you think you are is just a function of random accidents of birth and of upbringing. And you probably haven't actively chosen the type of person that you want to be. For example, if I was being myself, I would never be creating content on social media because my myself is like a shy, introvert kid who's like scared of what other people are going to think about him. But I'm so glad I got over that. I'm so glad I wasn't myself when I first started making YouTube videos back in 2017 because my life has been completely transformed as a result of not being myself. So yeah, broadly I just think this idea of be yourself is just a bit of a myth. Like what do people even mean when they say that? Oh, you know, you shouldn't be fake. You shouldn't be inauthentic. But like if you're trying to step out of your comfort zone in any capacity, it's going to feel weird initially. It's going to feel fake or inauthentic. If you're super shy and introverted and struggle to talk to anyone, the first time you talk to someone, it's going to feel weird, right? But that's not you not being yourself. It's you actively stepping outside of your comfort zone and choosing to develop a skill that you authentically want to develop. So that is why I really firmly don't believe in the advice of being yourself. I very much believe that you should instead choose yourself and you can mold your life in a direction that you actually want it to
