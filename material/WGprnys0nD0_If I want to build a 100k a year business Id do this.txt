All right. So, if I wanted to build a $100,000 a year business by the end of 2026 and I didn't have any resources to get started, this is what I would do. Phase one would be ideation. I would spend a little bit of time figuring out what is my niche. A niche is a combination of a group of people who have a particular problem. And what I'm doing is I'm hunting around for anyone that I know, anyone I have access to, anyone in my network, anyone I'm familiar with who firstly has money and secondly has painful problems that I could potentially solve with my business. For the most part, this is going to mean that I'm going to try and target rich people or businesses rather than students or consumers or normal people. And the problem that I'm going to try and solve is going to be something that helps that person make more money because generally you can charge a lot for helping someone else make more money. Once I figure that out, I would create a first draft offer. I'd figure out, okay, what is the product that I could potentially create, which would probably be a one-on-one service or a one-on-one consulting engagement that could help that person solve that problem to help them make more money. Phase two would then be validation. I would then take that out to the marketplace and I would try and do whatever I can to hustle for a handful of discovery calls where I pitch my product, my offer to that sort of person. As a result of taking these discovery calls, I would end up refining my offer. And hopefully after I've taken like somewhere between 10 and 20 calls, I would actually make my first sale. And I'd be making sure I charge at least $2,000, ideally $5,000 in a dream world, even $10,000 for the thing that I'm helping someone with. Once I've made my first sale, it's actually not that hard getting to $100,000 a year run rate because that's like $8,300 a month. So, let's say I was selling a package for like 3K a month. All I really need is three clients to sign up for 3K a month in order for me to have my $100,000 a year business. And so, I would really focus on doing an amazing job for the clients that I do have, getting referrals from those clients, doing content on social media every single day, probably on LinkedIn because that's tends to be where richer people hang out. And in the process of doing that, I would develop a lead generation mechanism to be able to get people into my business and a customer delivery mechanism to allow me to deliver great results for my clients. And then in no time, I would have a $100,000 a year
