Here are three financial tips that school probably didn't teach you. Number one, the only way to make money is to exchange it in return for solving someone else's problems. All money is an exchange for solving problems. Unless you're the bank or the Fed, you're not allowed to print money. Unless you want to go to jail, you cannot steal money. The only way for you to make money is for someone else to give it to you. And the only reason someone else would give you money is in exchange for you solving one of their problems. Start thinking like a problem hunting machine rather than thinking of yourself as like someone with a job or someone with a salary. You are someone who gets paid to solve problems. Secondly, the amount of money you make is directly proportional to how painful the problem you're solving is and how much money the person you are solving it for has. So, if you can find a way to build skills that allow you to solve more painful problems, or you can find a way to take your existing skills and use those to help people with more money, you are just way more likely to make money compared to the alternative. And thirdly, not all skills are created equal. There are certain skills that result in way higher income potentials than other skills. Unfortunately, in this capitalist world that we live in, having the skill of nursing is not a particularly highinccome skill. Having the skill of firefighting, again, not a particularly high income skill. Unfortunately, I wish it wasn't like that, but having the skill of knowing how to use AI, having the skill of knowing how to code, having the skill of knowing how to sell, having the skill of copywriting, generally if you can develop a skill that helps rich people get richer, unfortunately, that helps other businesses make more money, those kind of skills are far more likely to get you to financial freedom. And it's a real unfortunate fact of life that your value to society is actually not very correlated with your value to the free market.
