Title: What I learned being around millionaires
Video ID: XlyDWxCjjSo
URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XlyDWxCjjSo
Author: Ali Abdaal

================================================================================

I was recently at an event where everyone there was doing at least $10 million a year in revenue from their businesses. And I was like the brokeest person there. And so I was taking a lot of notes and I learned a lot of stuff. But one of the key things that I really took away from that was just how much faster these people moved compared to me and compared to other people who are trying to start businesses. Like a lot of these businesses were e-commerce businesses where they're sort of selling stuff and building brands to sell via Shopify or Amazon FBA. Now I know people who've tried to start those sorts of businesses. I even tried to start one myself. We spend months trying to research the right niche and then sort of off the product and all this sort of stuff. And then like eight months goes by. If we haven't like overthun the process or just quit by that point, maybe we release one thing then and kind of hope for the best. But actually a bunch of these guys who were doing like $10 to $50 million a year in sales. I was asking them about how they got started and they were just launching stuff so quickly. They were launching a new product every single week because their view was, well, I don't really know what's going to work. The market is going to tell me what works. So instead of spending ages trying to do the research, I might as well put the stuff out there and see what happens. And I was like, damn. This was a pattern that I noticed amongst loads of people that were at the event and they were doing talks at the event that they just had a bias to action. They were just doing stuff at a way, way, way faster cadence than I would have thought is reasonable and that most people I know who are trying to start businesses and failing at it are actually operating. And so when in doubt, if you're trying to build a business, it really seems that speed is the goal.
