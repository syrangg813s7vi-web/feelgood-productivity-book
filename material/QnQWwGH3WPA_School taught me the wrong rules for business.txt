Title: School taught me the wrong rules for business
Video ID: QnQWwGH3WPA
URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QnQWwGH3WPA
Author: Ali Abdaal

================================================================================

One thing that's often surprised me when I meet very successful entrepreneurs is just how many of them were actually pretty bad at school. They didn't follow instructions. They were disruptive, at least from what they tell me. They didn't particularly care about the exams. And I think what's interesting about this is that the mindset and the behaviors that it takes to be a good student are often quite different than the mindsets and behaviors it takes to be a good entrepreneur. If you're a good student like I was, you're optimizing for getting the right answer. You want to do loads of preparation. You want to do loads of thinking before you take any action. Whereas often the entrepreneurs that are the most successful are the ones who just sort of take the action anyway, iterate and adjust as they go along. This is something that I've noticed a lot with students in our lifestyle business academy, which is like our online business school. You know, they were all good students. And so there's a massive tendency to really really overthink things and really struggle to take imperfect action. Cuz when you're a good student, if you get like a B-grade or a C-grade, it like crushes your self-esteem and self-worth. And so similarly for these people who are starting out businesses, if someone says no to you on a sales call or says they don't want to buy your product or gives you a bad review or leaves a negative comment, oh my god, the crushing blow to the self-esteem can be difficult to deal with. And so it's just a thing that we got to get over.
